## Option 1 : Enrichir l'article actuel (version 2.0)

### Ajouts proposés

**Section "Three Types of Friction"**
```markdown
## Not All Friction is Useless

When AI removes friction, it reveals three distinct categories:

1. **Bullshit Friction**: Pure waste. Formatting reports no one reads, copying data between systems, administrative theater.
   - *Example*: Writing meeting minutes that get filed and never referenced
   - *AI impact*: Pure win. Good riddance.

2. **Necessary Friction**: The struggle IS the value.
   - *Example*: Learning to code by debugging vs. copy-pasting solutions
   - *AI impact*: Complex. Removes gatekeeping, but also removes growth.

3. **Ritual Friction**: No utilitarian payoff, but social/psychological value.
   - *Example*: Handwritten thank-you notes, elaborate code review processes
   - *AI impact*: Exposes what we were actually optimizing for (relationship, status, belonging).
```

**Section "The Three Responses"**
```markdown
## What Happens Next

When AI exposes the void, we split into three camps:

1. **The Eliminators**: "Finally! Let's automate everything without real value."
   - Risk: Throwing out ritual friction with bullshit friction

2. **The Nostalgics**: "The effort WAS the point! Bring back friction!"
   - Risk: Romanticizing waste, gatekeeping

3. **The Reframers**: "Now we can ask: what were we actually trying to do?"
   - Opportunity: Redesign work around actual value, not effort-as-proxy
```

## Option 3 : Angle alternatif - "The Effort Trap"

### Recentrer sur un angle plus nuancé

**Nouvelle thèse** : L'IA n'expose pas que les tâches sans valeur — elle expose notre confusion entre trois choses distinctes :
1. L'effort (input)
2. Le processus (transformation)
3. Le résultat (output)

**Structure proposée**
- We optimized for effort because it was measurable
- We confused "hard to do" with "valuable to have"
- We missed that some processes create value independent of outcome
- AI forces us to untangle these

**Plus philosophique, moins "hot take"**

## Option 5 : Le contre-article - "Laziness is Still Laziness"

### Devil's advocate position

**Thèse** : L'IA n'a rien révélé du tout. On le savait déjà. La flemme reste la flemme.

**Arguments**
- Bullshit jobs (David Graeber, 2013) : nothing new
- Parkinson's Law (1955) : work expands to fill time available
- On a toujours su quelles tâches étaient vides
- Ce qui a changé : on peut enfin les déléguer sans honte sociale

**Conclusion alternative** : Le problème n'était pas cognitif (cost vs. benefit), c'était social (si je ne fais pas ces tâches, quel est mon rôle ?).
